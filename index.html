PROJECTS

TITLE - Machine Learning Based Optimization of Machining AISI 1045 Steel
In Flood Cooling Environment

This study investigates the utilization of a high-speed conventional lathe and a coated carbide single point cutting tool facilitated machining experiments
on AISI1045 stainless steel,Focusing on surface roughness (Ra) and material removal rate (MRR) as important process characteristics.
The investigation involved three input parameters: cutting speed, feed, and depth of cut, each set at three distinct levels.
To streamline experimental efforts, a Taguchi L9 experimental design was adopted for the turning experiments.
Predictive models for Ra and MRR were developed using
Multiple Regression Analysis (MRA) in a Minitab software which have inbuilt Machine Learning
libraries, and subsequently, The Genetic algorithm was employed to optimize process parameters for
minimum Ra and maximum MRR. The outcomes of the single-variable optimizations were presented,
affirming the efficacy of the developed Response Surface Methodology (RSM) model, which
exhibited good accuracy and suitability for optimization purposes.
